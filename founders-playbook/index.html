02 / lifecycle

The four stages, explained simply

Each stage has a different question. Do not use AI to skip the question.

🔍

Idea

Question: is this a real problem?

Use AI for market research, competitor mapping, interview planning, and devil’s advocate thinking.

Exit whenYou can name who has the problem, how painful it is, and why current solutions fail.
🛠️

MVP

Question: what is the smallest useful product?

Use AI to code, test, write specs, and reduce scope. The goal is usage signal, not feature count.

Exit whenReal users repeat the behavior, pay, refer, or depend on it.
📣

Launch

Question: can we repeat growth?

Use AI for content, sales materials, onboarding docs, support workflows, analytics, and operating rhythm.

Exit whenYou have repeatable acquisition, activation, and retention signals.
🏰

Scale

Question: what becomes defensible?

Use AI to systematize knowledge, improve reliability, monitor quality, and automate company operations.

Exit whenThe business gets stronger from data, workflow integration, trust, and domain depth.
03 / teaching workflow

The safest path from idea to product

If you are new, follow this sequence. It protects you from building the wrong thing beautifully.

💭1. Hunch“I think this problem exists.”
→
📌2. HypothesisMake it specific: who, pain, frequency, current workaround.
→
🧪3. EvidenceTalk to users. Search for complaints. Ask AI to disprove you.
→
🧱4. Tiny MVPBuild only the part needed to test the behavior.
→
📊5. SignalLook for repeat usage, payment, referrals, or strong pull.
⚠️ Beginner mistake: “I built it, therefore it is validated.” A prototype is not proof. It is a prop for learning from users.

05 / traps

Six traps beginners should remember

These are the “watch out” signs. AI makes each one easier to fall into.

1. Building ≠ validation

A working demo proves you can build. It does not prove anyone needs it.

❌ “It works”
✅ “Users ask for it again”

2. Confirmation bias gets faster

If you ask AI to support your idea, it will. Ask it to attack your idea too.

❌ Supportive research only
✅ Disconfirming evidence

3. Scope creep feels free

Because features are cheap to generate, it is tempting to add too much.

❌ More features
✅ Smaller test

4. Agentic debt accumulates

Fast AI-generated work can create hidden mess: unclear specs, fragile code, and forgotten assumptions.

❌ “Ship and forget”
✅ Specs, tests, docs

5. Launch spike is not PMF

A burst of attention is not the same as repeat usage or willingness to pay.

❌ One big day
✅ Repeated behavior

6. The founder becomes the bottleneck

If every decision, workflow, and customer issue still needs you, you have not built a system.

❌ Founder handles all
✅ Systems handle routine
06 / concrete example

How to turn a vague idea into a testable hypothesis

This is the core beginner skill: make the problem specific enough to verify.

Too vague
“People struggle with expense reporting.”

This is hard to test. Which people? How often? Why does it hurt? What do they do today?

Testable
“Finance managers at mid-market companies spend 4+ hours each week reconciling expense submissions because their current tool does not integrate with accounting software.”

Now you can interview the right persona, check current workflow, measure pain, and design a tiny MVP.

07 / defensibility

The model is not the moat

Anyone can access strong AI models. Your advantage must come from what surrounds the model.

🧠 Domain knowledgeDeep understanding of users, jargon, exceptions, and taste.
📚 Proprietary contextDocuments, workflows, historical decisions, and customer-specific knowledge.
🧪 Evaluation testsReal-world scenarios that generic AI tools fail but your system handles.

08 / beginner checklist

What to do next if you have an idea

Use this as a simple operating checklist before you ask AI to build.

🎯
Write the hypothesisWho has the problem, how often, how painful, current workaround.
👿
Ask AI to attack itFind reasons it may be wrong, late, too small, or already solved.
🗺️
Map competitorsDirect, indirect, adjacent, and “do nothing” alternatives.
👥
Talk to five usersAsk about the last time they had the problem, not whether they like your idea.
🧱
Build the smallest testOnly build what proves or disproves the riskiest assumption.
📊
Measure pullRepeat usage, payment, referrals, switching pain, or strong urgency.
